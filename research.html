Research Interests

Exploring the Future of Wearable Electronics

My research focuses on three interconnected areas that push the boundaries of wearable electronics and human interaction technologies. Each area represents a critical component in creating the next generation of intelligent, adaptive systems.

Area 1

High Performance Wearable Sensors

Development of advanced wearable sensors based on functional structures and materials. These sensors leverage novel architectures and material properties to achieve superior sensitivity, flexibility, and biocompatibility for continuous health monitoring applications.

Irregular Microdome Structures

Pressure sensors using internal popping mechanisms for enhanced sensitivity

Piezo-transmittance Technology

Self-powered sensors integrated with organic solar cells for sustainable operation

Functional Materials

Novel material combinations for improved sensing performance and durability

Research Visualization

3D Electronics

Area 2

Manufacturing Technologies for 3D Electronics

Innovative fabrication methods to create three-dimensional electronic devices that conform to complex geometries. This research enables the production of stretchable, customizable electronics that can be seamlessly integrated with curved surfaces and dynamic objects.

Predistorted Pattern Generation

Advanced algorithms for creating optimized 2D patterns that transform into 3D structures

Thermoforming Techniques

Controlled deformation processes for precise 3D shaping of electronic devices

Conformal Electronics

Stretchable circuits that maintain functionality across complex surface topographies

Area 3

HCI and HRI Applications

Creating practical applications that bridge human and machine through wearable electronics. This research translates sensor technologies and fabrication methods into real-world systems for healthcare monitoring, assistive devices, and interactive interfaces.

Health Monitoring Systems

Continuous vital sign tracking using non-invasive wearable sensors

Interactive Interfaces

Gesture recognition and tactile feedback systems for intuitive human-computer interaction

Assistive Technologies

Wearable devices that enhance human capabilities and support rehabilitation
