Customizable, Conformal, 3D-Shaped Electronics

Development of customizable, conformal, and stretchable electronic systems seamlessly integrated onto complex three-dimensional surfaces. This research focuses on advanced fabrication strategies that enable reliable operation of electronics on curved and deformable structures for wearable and robotic platforms.

Stretchable Physical Sensors

Development of high-performance stretchable physical sensors for detecting pressure, strain, and multi-axial mechanical stimuli. This research emphasizes novel sensor structures, material design, and stable electrical performance under large mechanical deformation.

Paper J. Choi et al., Nano Energy, 2020

Tactile Sensor Arrays for Wearables and Robotics

Development of tactile sensor arrays and integrated sensing systems that provide spatially resolved and multi-modal tactile information for wearable devices and robotic platforms. This research bridges sensor array design, signal readout, and system-level integration to enable human-like tactile perception.

Paper H. Han et al., npj Flex. Electron., 2025

Multi-Sensor Systems for HCI/HRI

Design and implementation of integrated multi-sensor systems for interactive applications, including human–computer interaction (HCI), human–robot interaction (HRI), wearable interfaces, and intelligent sensing platforms. This research focuses on system integration, data interpretation, and real-world deployment.
